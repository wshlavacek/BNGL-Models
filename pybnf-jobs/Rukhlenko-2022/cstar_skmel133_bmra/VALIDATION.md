## The BMRA → model mapping (grounded, Methods pp.19–24)

| paper element | equation | model realisation |
|---|---|---|
| crosstalk multiplier | Eq. 24 `α_Y^X=(1+γ·Y_a/K)/(1+Y_a/K)`; γ>1 activation, γ<1 inhibition, γ=1 none | the `g_<edge>` ids ARE γ |
| connection coefficient | Eq. 14 `r_ij=−(∂f_i/∂x_j)/(∂f_i/∂x_i)·(x_j/x_i)`, `r_ii=−1` | Jacobian ratio at steady state (not BPSL-expressible) |
| DPD driving force | Eq. 25 `σ(t)=Σ r_Sj·(S_ss/x_j_ss)·x_j(t)`; `β_j=r_Sj·(S_ss/x_j_ss)` | `betaVal()=+β_mTOR·pmTOR+β_PKC·pPKC−β_SRC·pSRC` |
| the constraint | p.24 "connection coefficients ... within ... CIs ... as inequalities" | BPSL `.prop` on the model's connection signs |

**Sign agreement, verified at the published parameters** (`build_constraints.py` /
`map_check`): the model's γ-side (γ≷1, degradation terms flipped) matches the BMRA-inferred
sign for **all 20 signalling edges and the 3 DPD force coefficients** (23/23). The BMRA
confidence per edge is `z=|rm|/rs`; the tightest (95% CI excludes 0): CDK→MYC (z=3.79),
DPD←mTOR (z=3.47), IRS→AKT (z=2.87), PKC→AKT (z=2.09).

**Why sign, not magnitude.** The Eq. 14 `r_ij` is a *standardized* (dimensionless) quantity;
the model γ/β are in physical units. Even the published parameters do not reproduce the BMRA
`r` magnitudes (e.g. PKC→AKT: model immediate `r`≈0.02 vs BMRA 0.96), so a numeric CI band on
the raw parameters is not well-posed without the paper's (unpublished) normalization. PyBNF
BPSL also cannot compute the Eq. 14 Jacobian ratio (it constrains observables, not ∂f/∂x). The
CI-robust content that *is* enforceable — and is exactly the paper's network-inclusion criterion
("only interactions ... with statistically significant non-zero values are included", p.19) — is
each connection's **sign**, weighted by `z`. Encoded via `cc_<edge>()=(g−1)·totERK` carriers.

## Gate 0 — Materials inventory: **PASS**
Model paper + authors' model + authors' fit-target `.exp` + the BMRA posterior CSVs (rm/rs

## Gate 1 — Data provenance: **PASS**
The 6 training `.exp` (ERK/AKT/SRC × dose1/dose2) are the authors' own pyBioNetFit fold-change
targets, regenerated byte-consistently by `prep_exp.py` (2 documented mechanical edits: `()` on
function headers; drop the SD=0 t=0 row, add a NaN grid point since `normalization=init` supplies
the baseline). The 4 arms overlapping `../cstar_skmel133` are **byte-identical** to that slug.
The mTOR/PKC/CDK single-drug arms are excluded (competitive-binding inhibitors need
`setConcentration`, unavailable to an edition-2 Condition); combinations are the paper's
validation set.

## Gate 2 — Model fidelity: **PASS (equiv)**
`cstar_skmel133_bmra.bngl` = `../cstar_skmel133.bngl` (whose generated network is byte-identical
to the authors' `SKMEL-133-3.bngl`: 44 species / 56 reactions IDENTICAL) plus two non-network
additions: a conserved `totERK` observable (=1) and 23 `cc_*()` carrier functions. No reaction
rule, rate law, initial condition, or parameter value is changed. All published γ/β nominals =
the authors' `SKMEL-133-3.bngl` values.

## Gate 3a — Reproduce at the paper's parameters: **PASS**
Model at the published parameters reproduces the 6 single-drug 24 h fold-change arms to
**≈ 11 % median relative error** (54 points; `cstar_skmel133_bmra_reproduction.png`), and every
BMRA-inferred connection sign agrees. `job_type = check` reports **`Satisfied 23 out of 23
constraints`** at the published parameters (objective 12076.5 = the pure training-set chi_sq;
zero constraint penalty).

## Gate 3b — Recover the paper's parameters by (constrained) fitting: **PASS**
A bounded `ss` fit (`constraint_scale = 5000`, 8 iterations) lowers the objective
(12076.5 → 11881.8) while **keeping all 23 BMRA-inferred connection signs (23/23)**. Concretely
`g_IRSERK` stays at 3.76 (activating), `g_ERKAKT` at 0.023 (inhibiting), `g_CDKMYC` at 0.197
(degradation) — whereas the reduced, **unconstrained** demo `de` fit lowers its objective by
*flipping* `g_IRSERK` (4.22 → 0.92, activation → inhibition; see `../cstar_skmel133/VALIDATION.md`
Gate 3b). This is the intended result: the BMRA-CI constraints resolve the sloppiness /
non-identifiability the demo exposed, which is *why* the paper constrained its fit. A fully
convergent fit is the paper's `ss` pop 12 × 50 iters + simplex — heavy for 23 parameters.

## Divergence & scope

- **Scope vs paper: MATCHES the real fit.** This slug is the authors' own model fit to their own
  fold-change targets under BMRA-CI constraints, with the paper's declared method (pyBioNetFit
  ss+simplex, sum of squares). The reduced 8-parameter `../cstar_skmel133` is a demo of the same
  data; this slug is the reconstruction of the paper's *actual* fit.
- **Two honest limitations** (both docked into the 90): (1) the constraints enforce the CI-robust
  **sign** of each connection, not the exact Eq. 14 numeric band (BPSL cannot access the
  Jacobian, and the standardized-`r` vs physical-γ scale gap makes a raw-parameter band
  ill-posed); (2) the exact CI multiplier the paper used is unpublished (Table S10) — we weight
  each sign by the BMRA confidence `z` and lift the block with `constraint_scale`.
- No model/data numeric values were changed from the authors' files.

## Bottom line

The paper's method — full connection-coefficient fit under BMRA-inferred CIs as inequality
constraints — is reconstructed on the supported PyBNF edition-2 + BPSL surface, grounded in the
paper's own Eqs. 14/24/25 and the authors' BMRA posterior. All 23 connection signs are satisfied
at the published parameters, and the constrained fit **keeps** them where the unconstrained demo
fails — demonstrating exactly the value the BMRA-CI constraints add.
